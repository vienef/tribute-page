Ada Lovelace

  • 1833 Ada Lovelace met Charles Babbage for the first time. After the first meeting, Charles Babbage invited her to see the Difference Engine. She was fascinated by the machine.
  • 1842 Ada Lovelace translated an article on Babbage's Analytical Engine by Luigi Menabrea. She appended notes explaining how the Analytical Engine differed from the Difference Engine.
  • 1844 Ada Lovelace worked on other projects after her work with Babbage. She commented to a friend about her desire to build a mathematical model ("a calculus of the nervous system").
  • 1851 Ada Lovelace wrote to her mother, mentioning she was working on the relationship between maths and music.
